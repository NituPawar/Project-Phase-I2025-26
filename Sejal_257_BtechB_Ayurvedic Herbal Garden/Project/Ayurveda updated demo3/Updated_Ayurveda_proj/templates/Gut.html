Gut & Intestinal Health – Ayurvedic Support

Ayurveda emphasizes digestive health as the foundation of overall wellness. Imbalances in Agni (digestive fire) or excess Ama (toxins) can lead to bloating, constipation, acidity, diarrhea, or poor nutrient absorption. Ayurvedic herbs, diet, and lifestyle practices help restore digestive balance and promote gut health.

1. Triphala

  • Regulates bowel movements and detoxifies the intestines
  • Improves digestion and nutrient absorption
  • Balances Vata and Pitta doshas

Usage: Take 1 tsp Triphala powder with warm water at bedtime.

2. Ginger (Adrak)

  • Enhances digestive fire (Agni)
  • Reduces bloating, nausea, and gas

Usage: Drink fresh ginger tea or add ginger to meals.

3. Fennel Seeds (Saunf)

  • Soothes the stomach and reduces acidity
  • Improves digestion and prevents gas formation

Usage: Chew fennel seeds after meals or drink fennel tea.

4. Cumin, Coriander & Carom Seeds (Jeera, Dhania, Ajwain)

  • Relieves indigestion, bloating, and flatulence
  • Strengthens digestive fire

Usage: Add these seeds to food or consume as herbal decoction.

5. Aloe Vera

  • Soothes intestinal inflammation
  • Supports regular bowel movements
  • Balances Pitta and Kapha doshas

Usage: Drink 1–2 tsp fresh aloe vera juice before meals.

6. Dietary Recommendations

  • Eat warm, freshly cooked, easily digestible foods
  • Include whole grains, vegetables, ghee, and herbal soups
  • Avoid processed, oily, and cold/raw foods in excess

7. Lifestyle & Yoga

  • Practice mindful eating and chew food properly
  • Drink warm water throughout the day
  • Do gentle yoga and abdominal massage to improve digestion
  • Practice Pranayama like Kapalabhati and Anulom Vilom
